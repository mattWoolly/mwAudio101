# Source Mixer, Modulation Grip (bender) & Portamento

## 1. Scope and summary

This document is the research source of truth for three coupled subsystems of
the Roland SH-101 as they pertain to the mwAudio101 clone: the SOURCE MIXER
(independent simultaneous levels for saw, pulse/square, sub-oscillator and
noise), the pitch/modulation BENDER lever (plus the optional MGS-1/MG-1
Modulation Grip and the LFO-trigger gesture), and PORTAMENTO (glide). It is a
permanent, citable reference; later architecture and backlog phases cite it by
section number.

The SH-101 SOURCE MIXER is an **additive (summing) mixer, not a switched
selector**: it has independent level knobs for the VCO pulse/square wave, the
VCO sawtooth, the sub-oscillator, and noise, all of which mix simultaneously
"in various proportions" before feeding the VCF [owner's manual, high]
[ref:1][ref:2][ref:3]. The sub-oscillator is a 4013 CMOS dual flip-flop
frequency divider driven from the VCO ramp, offering three switch-selectable
shapes: -1 octave square, -2 octave square, and a -2 octave narrow pulse
[reverse-engineered: Electric Druid, high][ref:4]. Pitch/modulation is handled
by a spring-loaded bender lever plus three slider knobs (VCO Bend Sens, VCF
Bend Sens, LFO MOD depth) and an LFO MOD switch on the lever; the lever bends
pitch/filter side-to-side and, pushed back/forward, routes the LFO triangle
waveform to VCO (vibrato) and VCF (growl), maxing at ~1200 cents for the VCO
[owner's manual, high][ref:1][ref:3]. The optional MGS-1/MG-1 grip duplicates
these via an upward-only bender and an LFO-Mod button [owner's manual, high]
[ref:1][ref:5]. Portamento is a glide with OFF/ON/AUTO modes and a TIME knob of
0–5 s; AUTO glides only on legato playing and glide is disabled during
arpeggio [owner's manual, high][ref:1]. The shared LFO/modulator covers
0.1–30 Hz [service-manual, high][ref:6][ref:1].

## 2. Source mixer

### 2.1 Additive summing topology

The SOURCE MIXER is an additive summing mixer with independent, simultaneous
level controls — it is **not** a waveform selector switch. The owner's manual
SOURCE MIXER section states: "The SOURCE MIXER mixes the VCO {pulse or saw},
SUB Oscillator, and Noise in various proportions, before sending them to the
VCF" [owner's manual, high][ref:1]. The VCO section separately notes that the
two VCO waveforms (pulse and saw) "are sent to the SOURCE MIXER and mixed at
any portion you like," confirming saw and pulse are mixed independently, not
switched [owner's manual, high][ref:1]. Roland's own blog calls it "four sound
sources played per one note... each sound blended via the SOURCE MIXER"
[ref:3], and Wikipedia describes "1 VCO with 3 simultaneously mixable
waveforms: Pulse with PWM, Saw and Sub wave" [ref:2].

This claim is verified `confirmed` against the owner's manual SOURCE MIXER
section and the Roland Cloud plug-out manual, both of which list the same level
controls [ref:1][ref:7].

### 2.2 Level controls

The mixer exposes five controls [owner's manual, high][ref:1][ref:7]:

1. Pulse/Square Level knob
2. Saw Level knob
3. SUB Oscillator Level knob
4. SUB Oscillator Waveform selector switch
5. Noise Level knob

The pulse path carries its own PWM-modulated width upstream of the mixer; the
mixer itself sets only the relative blend levels.

### 2.3 Sub-oscillator: shapes and divider circuit

The SUB Oscillator Waveform selector offers three switch-selectable shapes:
-1 octave square, -2 octave square, and a -2 octave narrow pulse. The owner's
manual spec table lists "1 OCT Down / 2 OCT Down / 2 OCT Down" (square, square,
pulse), and the Roland Cloud SH-101 manual labels them explicitly as "1 OCT
DOWN: One octave below", "2 OCT DOWN: Two octave below", and "2 OCT DOWN: Two
octave below (narrow width)" [owner's manual + Roland Cloud manual, high]
[ref:1][ref:7]. The owner's manual also states the sub-oscillator "generates
the output signal one octave or two lower than the VCO's. The output waveform
of this Oscillator is Pulse Wave" [owner's manual, high][ref:1].

The sub-oscillator is generated by a **4013 CMOS dual flip-flop** dividing the
VCO sawtooth/ramp by 2 and by 4; the -2 octave "narrow pulse" is a diode-OR
combination of the two divider outputs, giving a pulse that is high 75% of the
time and low 25% of the time (i.e. a 25% duty cycle)
[reverse-engineered: Electric Druid][ref:4]. Electric Druid's sub-oscillator
circuit study states the SH-101 uses "a CMOS 4013 dual bistable chip" to make
"square at -1 octave, square at -2 octaves, and 25% pulse at -2 octaves,"
derived by processing the main oscillator's ramp through flip-flop dividers,
and that the 25% pulse is formed "using a couple of diodes as a diode-OR
circuit. If either flip-flop output is high, the OR's output is high" [ref:4].

> **Honest label.** The octave/shape topology (1 oct square, 2 oct square,
> 2 oct narrow pulse) is `confirmed` by the Roland Cloud manual and Wikipedia
> [ref:7][ref:2]. The **specific 25% duty-cycle figure** for the narrow pulse
> is attributed only to the Electric Druid teardown — the owner's manual and
> Roland Cloud manual say only "narrow width" with no number. Verification
> rated the 25% figure `uncertain` (single secondary source, not
> manufacturer-documented). Treat 25% as
> [reverse-engineered: Electric Druid, presumed] until confirmed against a
> primary schematic [ref:4].

### 2.4 Summing-node circuit (undocumented)

The exact source-mixer summing topology is **not documented** in any consulted
text source. This includes: which op-amp/IC forms the summing node, the
per-source summing resistor values, whether the IR3109 VCF input pin doubles as
the mix bus, the sub-oscillator level taper, and whether the summed output
soft-clips/saturates at high combined levels or stays linear
[theory/inference, unmeasured].

The owner's manual and Roland Cloud manual describe only the function and the
four level knobs — no op-amp detail, no resistor values, no clipping/headroom
figures [owner's manual + Roland Cloud manual, high][ref:1][ref:7]. The SH-101
service manual is a scanned/image-based PDF from which `pdftotext` extracted
zero machine-readable text, so its schematic component values could not be
machine-read in this research pass [ref:8][ref:9]. Per the frozen schematic
reconciliation, the IR3109 (IC14) is the VCF/VCA chip and the CEM3340 (IC13) is
the VCO; **neither** is documented as the mixer summing element. Electric
Druid's IR3109 article notes the chip runs single-supply 9V/GND with ~+5V as a
virtual ground that mixer components reference, but that is component-level
theory/observation, **not** an SH-101 source-mixer headroom spec
[clone-derived: Alfa AS3109/Electric Druid, theory][ref:10].

This claim was rated `low` confidence and `disputed: true` in research and
`confirmed` in verification only in the sense that the documentation gap itself
is real — the topology is genuinely undocumented at the text level, not that
any specific topology is established (see Section 7).

## 3. Bender lever and modulation routing

### 3.1 Controls and axes

Pitch/modulation is controlled by a spring-loaded BENDER lever plus three
slider knobs and an LFO MOD switch built into the lever [owner's manual, high]
[ref:1][ref:3]:

- VCO Bend Sens knob
- VCF Bend Sens knob
- LFO MOD (a.k.a. LFO-VMOD / Modulation Depth) knob
- Bender lever with built-in LFO MOD switch

The bender axes are [owner's manual, high][ref:1][ref:3]:

- **Side-to-side** = pitch/filter bend, scaled by the VCO Bend Sens and
  VCF Bend Sens knobs.
- **Pushed back/forward** (or holding the LFO MOD switch) = routes the LFO
  triangle output to the VCO (vibrato effect) and VCF (growl effect), scaled
  by the LFO MOD depth knob.

The owner's manual states: "If the BENDER lever is being pushed back, LFO
{triangle} output controls the VCO (vibrato effect) or VCF (growl effect). This
knob controls the depth of the effect" [owner's manual, high][ref:1]. Both the
axis behavior and the LFO MOD switch gesture are verified `confirmed` against
the owner's manual [ref:1].

### 3.2 Bend sensitivity (depths)

The maximum VCO bend depth from the bender is **~1200 cents (one octave)**. The
owner's manual VCO Bend Sens description reads: "This slider knob sets the
maximum effect of the BENDER when it is controlling the pitch of the VCO. (Max.
~1200 cents)" [owner's manual, high][ref:1]. Verified `confirmed`; 1200 cents =
one octave [ref:1].

The VCF Bend Sens knob "sets the maximum effect of the BENDER when it is
controlling the pitch [cutoff] of the VCF," but **no numeric cents/Hz range is
given** in the manual, unlike the VCO bender [owner's manual, high][ref:1].
This asymmetry is verified `confirmed`: the manual specifies a numeric max only
for the VCO bender and leaves VCF bend depth as a qualitative "maximum effect"
[ref:1]. The exact VCF bend max in octaves/Hz is an open question (Section 6).

For context only — and **not** part of the bend path — the global TUNE control
range is separately stated as ±50 cents [owner's manual, high][ref:1].

### 3.3 The shared LFO/modulator routed by the bender

The bender's "pushed-back" gesture routes the smooth LFO waveform. The
MODULATOR consists of the LFO and the S/H (Sample & Hold) and covers a rate of
**0.1–30 Hz** (shared with the clock) [service-manual, high][ref:6][ref:1]. The
WAVEFORM selector has four positions [owner's manual + Wikipedia, high]
[ref:1][ref:2]:

- Triangle
- Square
- Random (the output of the S/H — i.e. sample & hold)
- Noise

> **Honest label — triangle, not sine.** The smooth (non-square) LFO waveform
> on the original **hardware** is a **triangle**, not a sine. Wikipedia and
> Vintage Synth Explorer both list "triangle, square, random and noise"
> [ref:2][ref:11]. Decisively, the Roland Cloud SH-101 plug-out's MODULATOR
> section lists "S (Triangle wave)" as the smooth shape and separately adds
> "R (Sine wave)" as an **additional software-only waveform** [ref:7]. The
> "sine" label that appears in some Roland marketing/blog material is a
> software-era artifact (the Roland Cloud plug-out adds a sine option that the
> original hardware does not have); any claim that the original SH-101 LFO
> smooth wave is a sine is incorrect [ref:3][ref:7]. Verification upgraded this
> from `medium` to `high` confidence for the hardware. Caveat: the original
> owner's-manual OCR rendered the LFO waveform pictograms as garbled glyphs,
> so the word "triangle" is not literally printed in the manual's LFO text —
> the identification rests on the waveform diagram shape plus the corroborating
> spec tables [ref:1][ref:2][ref:11].

## 4. MGS-1 / MG-1 Modulation Grip

The optional MGS-1/MG-1 Modulation Grip provides the same bender + LFO-Mod
functions for keytar-style play [owner's manual, high][ref:1][ref:5]:

- **Bender (upward-only).** The grip's bender "changes the tone color and the
  pitch, but works only in upper direction (to higher pitch and brighter tone
  color)" — described as a "guitar choking-like sound" [owner's manual, high]
  [ref:1]. Note this upward-only behavior describes the **grip's** bender; the
  SH-101's own panel bender lever remains bidirectional (side-to-side pitch).
- **LFO Modulation button.** Pressing it "you can obtain the same effect as
  produced by pushing the Bender lever of the SH-101, i.e. LFO {triangle}
  waveform modulates the VCO (vibrato) and VCF (growl). The intensity of the
  modulation is adjusted with the Modulation Depth knob in the Controllers
  section" [owner's manual, high][ref:1].

When the grip is connected via the Modulation Grip jack, the SH-101's own
bender lever continues to work in parallel [owner's manual, high][ref:1]. Both
the upward-only direction and the LFO button equivalence are verified
`confirmed` against the owner's manual; the MGS-1 (the modern reissue grip) is
corroborated by the Make Noise product listing [ref:1][ref:5].

## 5. Portamento (glide)

Portamento has a TIME knob with range **0–5 s** and a three-position MODE
switch [owner's manual + service-manual, high][ref:1][ref:6]:

- **OFF** — portamento effect is not available at all.
- **ON** — portamento effect is always obtained.
- **AUTO** — portamento effect is obtained only when playing legato (releasing
  the key before pressing the next key).

The owner's manual states: "PORTAMENTO Time knob — Portamento is a slide from
one pitch to another. This PORTAMENTO knob determines the time required to
change pitches when different keys are pressed" [owner's manual, high][ref:1].
Critically, "When an Arpeggio is being played, the portamento effect does not
work" — glide is **disabled while the arpeggiator runs** [owner's manual, high]
[ref:1].

The 0–5 s range is verified `confirmed` against two independent primary reads
(service-manual spec table "Portamento Time (0 ~ 5s)" plus the owner's manual)
[ref:6][ref:1]. The OFF/ON/AUTO modes and the arpeggio-disable behavior are
verified `confirmed` against the owner's manual, with the Roland Cloud manual
corroborating the OFF/AUTO/ON labels and the AUTO=legato meaning [ref:1][ref:7].

> **Honest label — glide curve unknown.** The manual gives only the 0–5 s time
> range. The glide **curve shape** (linear vs. exponential/RC) and whether the
> glide is constant-time or constant-rate is **not** documented. It is most
> likely an RC integrator on the pitch CV, but that is
> [theory/inference, unmeasured] and must be confirmed from the glide-circuit
> schematic (Section 6).

## 7. Confidence, disputes & honest labels

This section surfaces, plainly, every disputed/low-confidence item and residual
risk for this dimension.

### 7.1 Source-mixer summing topology is undocumented (low confidence, disputed)

The op-amp/IC forming the summing node, the per-source summing resistor values,
whether the IR3109 (IC14) VCF input pin doubles as the mix bus, and any
mix-bus clipping/headroom behavior are **not documented** in any consulted text
source [theory/inference, unmeasured]. The owner's and Roland Cloud manuals
describe only the level knobs [ref:1][ref:7]. The SH-101 service manual is a
scanned image PDF; `pdftotext` extracted zero text, so schematic component
values could not be machine-read in this pass [ref:8][ref:9]. **Do not claim
specific resistor values or a headroom/clipping figure without reading that
schematic.** The schematic almost certainly shows the values but requires
human/OCR-image reading.

### 7.2 Mix-bus clipping / headroom is theory only

Whether the summed output soft-clips at high combined levels (e.g. all four
sources at max) is analog-synth theory unless extracted from the schematic plus
the IR3109/AS3109 datasheet. Any such statement must be labelled
[theory/inference, unmeasured], not documented spec. The IR3109 single-supply
9V/GND, ~+5V virtual-ground detail is
[clone-derived: Alfa AS3109/Electric Druid, theory], not an original-instrument
measurement [ref:10].

### 7.3 Sub-oscillator 25% duty-cycle figure (uncertain)

The octave/shape topology (1 oct square, 2 oct square, 2 oct narrow pulse) is
`confirmed` [ref:7][ref:2], but the specific **25% duty-cycle** figure is
attributed only to the Electric Druid secondary source and was not re-confirmed
against a primary datasheet/schematic. Verification rated it `uncertain`. Label
it [reverse-engineered: Electric Druid, presumed] [ref:4].

### 7.4 Smooth LFO waveform: triangle (hardware) vs. sine (software)

The original **hardware** smooth LFO waveform is a **triangle**; "sine" is a
Roland Cloud software plug-out addition (the plug-out lists both a triangle and
a separate sine; the hardware has only the triangle) [ref:2][ref:7][ref:11].
Verification resolved the prior "sine vs triangle" dispute in favor of triangle
and upgraded confidence from medium to high for the hardware. Any doc text
calling the original SH-101 LFO smooth wave a sine is incorrect and is a
software-emulation artifact. Caveat: the owner's-manual OCR rendered the LFO
pictograms as garbled glyphs, so "triangle" is not literally printed in the
manual's LFO text; the identification rests on the diagram shape plus secondary
spec tables [ref:1].

### 7.5 VCF bend sensitivity has no numeric maximum (medium confidence)

Unlike the VCO bend (~1200 cents), the manual gives only a qualitative "maximum
effect" for the VCF bend with no cents/Hz figure [ref:1]. The exact VCF bend
max in octaves/Hz is an open validation gap.

### 7.6 Portamento glide curve is unknown

The manual gives only the 0–5 s time range. The curve (linear vs.
exponential/RC) and constant-time vs. constant-rate behavior are not documented
and are [theory/inference, unmeasured]; an RC integrator is the likely but
unconfirmed implementation.

### 7.7 Source-fetch caveats (residual risks)

- The official Roland support and Sweetwater spec pages returned HTTP 403 and
  could not be fetched; the manufacturer's exact LFO-waveform wording was
  corroborated via Wikipedia, Vintage Synth Explorer, and the owner's manual
  rather than the live Roland page [ref:2][ref:11][ref:1].
- The service manual PDF is image-only (zero extractable text), so all
  schematic-level facts in this dimension (summing node, glide curve, LFO
  waveshaper) remain open until the schematic is read by image/OCR
  [ref:8][ref:9].

### 7.8 Open validation gaps (no physical-unit measurements)

Per project decision, mwAudio101 has no physical-unit (bench) measurements.
Therefore the following are **OPEN VALIDATION GAPS**, not delivered facts:
oscilloscope-confirmed sub-oscillator duty cycle, measured mixer headroom/
clipping curves, and oscilloscope portamento glide curves. They must be
labelled as gaps wherever they would otherwise be stated as measured fact.

## 8. Design implications for mwAudio101

- **Source mixer = linear summing node, not a selector.** Model the SOURCE
  MIXER as a 4-input linear summing node with independent per-source gains: saw
  level, pulse/square level (the pulse path carries its own PWM-modulated
  width), sub-osc level, and noise level — all summed simultaneously. Do
  **not** implement saw/pulse as a mutually-exclusive selector [ref:1].
- **Sub-oscillator generator.** Implement as a divide-by-2 / divide-by-4 of the
  VCO core phase (model the 4013 flip-flops off the VCO ramp), with three
  selectable shapes: -1 oct 50% square, -2 oct 50% square, and -2 oct 25% pulse
  (the latter as a diode-OR / logic-OR of the two divider outputs, giving
  75%-high) [ref:4]. Keep the 25% figure flagged as Electric-Druid-attributed
  (Section 7.3).
- **Bender / mod = 2-axis controller.** Implement a horizontal pitch/filter
  bend whose depth is set by independent VCO Bend Sens (clamp at ±1200 cents)
  and VCF Bend Sens scalers, plus a "forward/back" axis (or a momentary LFO MOD
  switch) that applies the **triangle** LFO to VCO pitch and VCF cutoff scaled
  by the LFO MOD depth knob [ref:1]. Use a triangle, not a sine, for the smooth
  LFO (Section 7.4).
- **MGS-1 grip mapping.** Map the grip to the same engine but with an
  upward-only pitch/tone bend and a momentary LFO-Mod button [ref:1].
- **Portamento.** Implement an RC-style glide on the pitch CV with TIME mapped
  to 0–5 s, three modes (OFF / always-ON / AUTO=legato-only), and forced off
  while the arpeggiator/sequencer drives notes [ref:1]. Treat the exact glide
  curve as a TODO until the schematic is read (Section 7.6).
- **TODO before claiming analog accuracy.** Treat the source-mixer summing-node
  resistor values, any mix-bus clipping, the precise glide curve, and the exact
  LFO smooth-wave shape verification as open items to verify against the
  service-manual schematics (image/OCR read of the relevant pages) before
  finalizing analog-accuracy. None of these are delivered facts (Section 7).
